About

I study multiphase atmospheric chemistry with a focus on aqueous and heterogeneous oxidation pathways controlling HOx and peroxide budgets. My work bridges laboratory kinetic experiments, 0-D modeling, and analysis of field campaigns across urban and marine environments.

Research

HOx budgets in multiphase systems

Using the PKU-MARK kinetic framework, we quantify HO₂ uptake and condensed-phase peroxide production across aerosol and cloud-relevant regimes. The parameterization aligns with recommended IUPAC kinetics and is evaluated against chamber/flow-tube constraints.
Methods: box/0-D modeling · uptake parameterization · sensitivity analysis

Radicals detection by ESR and LIF

We detect OH, superoxide and carbonate radicals in aerosol extracts under simulated sunlight, and combine LIF-based HO₂ measurements in an FEP-coated flow tube to constrain uptake coefficients for metal-doped sulfate aerosols.
Methods: ESR · LIF · flow tube · metal-catalyzed reactions

Observation–model integration

Multi-site field campaigns (urban/marine) are combined with constrained chemical boxes to diagnose ROx production budgets, evaluate multiphase pathways, and assess implications for SOA aging and oxidant cycles.
Methods: field observations · constrained box models · radical budgets
